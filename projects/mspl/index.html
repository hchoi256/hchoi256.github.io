Overview of MSPL: offline multi-step pseudo-label generation and online open-vocabulary detector training.

Abstract

Open-vocabulary object detection (OVD) aims to recognize and localize object categories beyond the training set. Recent approaches leverage vision-language models to generate pseudo-labels using image-text alignment, allowing detectors to generalize to unseen classes without explicit supervision. However, these methods depend heavily on single-step image-text matching, neglecting the intermediate reasoning steps crucial for interpreting semantically complex visual contexts, such as crowding or occlusion. In this paper, we introduce MSPL, a framework that incorporates multi-step visual reasoning into the pseudo-labeling process for OVD. It decomposes complex scene understanding into three interpretable steps—object localization, category recognition, and background grounding—where these intermediate reasoning states serve as rich supervision sources. Extensive experiments on standard OVD evaluation protocols demonstrate that MSPL achieves state-of-the-art performance with superior pseudo-labeling efficiency, outperforming the strong baseline by 9.4 AP50 for novel classes on OV-COCO and improving box and mask APr by 3.2 and 2.2, respectively, on OV-LVIS.

Intro

Open-vocabulary detectors are expected to recognize novel categories using only base-class annotations. Recent pseudo-labeling approaches automate novel-class supervision with vision-language models, but they often rely on single-step image-text matching.

This single-step assignment becomes fragile in complex scenes: labels can be biased by surrounding context, objects missing from captions remain undiscovered, and unlabeled or occluded objects may collapse into the background. MSPL addresses these issues by turning pseudo-labeling into an explicit multi-step reasoning process.

Single-step pseudo-labeling can suffer from noisy boxes, caption dependency, and background collapse in crowded or occluded scenes.

Method

MSPL separates pseudo-label generation from detector training. In the offline stage, object-level regions are verified, assigned open-vocabulary labels, and grounded as foreground or background through structured MLLM reasoning.

In the online stage, the detector learns from the resulting pseudo-labels, region-level descriptions, and background concepts. Region-Text Alignment improves semantic supervision, while Contrastive Background Learning helps separate foreground objects from background embeddings.

Offline stage: MSPL generates refined pseudo-labels through object verification, category recognition, and background grounding.

Online stage: multi-step supervision is used for open-vocabulary detector training and discarded at inference.

Results

MSPL achieves strong open-vocabulary detection performance on standard OVD benchmarks. On OV-COCO, it reaches 43.4 APN50 with RN50 and 47.8 APN50 with RN50×4 under pseudo-annotation supervision.

On OV-LVIS, MSPL improves rare-category detection and segmentation, showing that multi-step pseudo-label supervision scales to large-vocabulary settings.
